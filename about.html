Oh, the places you'll go! Where I've been in the world.

The summer after freshman year my twin sister Jaden and I packed our 40L backpacks and hung out in Europe/the Middle East/Asia for four months. Since then, I haven't been able to stop seeking out that feeling of being an explorer, learning foreign swear words and touring local supermarkets (my favorite attractions). I've been very lucky to have had quite a few opportunities to travel for school-related business all over the continent (and now the world)!

Here are some of my favorite travel memories so far:

  • sitting on the ponte alla carraia eating gelato from this shop in florence
  • subathing on the beach in tel aviv
  • napping on the benches at the national archives in paris
  • eating through the ningxia night market in taipei
  • horseback riding during a thunderstorm in the cuban countryside
  • watching home videos from the 60s of Taipei with our host in Rome, Romano
  • making white toast and tuna fish sandwiches in tokyo
  • sunset hiking in cappadocia
  • feeding the swans in that river in zurich
  • eating the most delicious orange filo cake (portokalopita) at this restaurant in naxos

As you can see the list is ever-growing. :)

more about music

I'm classically trained and certified by the Royal Conservatory of Music in piano and have been playing since the age of 4. I have a preference for Bach and a life goal is to learn all of the Well-Tempered Clavier. Two volumes of 24 preludes and fugues means 96 pieces, well, I'm 4/96 of the way there.

Other instruments I played:

  • pipe organ -- yes, I played this and also the Hallelujah Chorus for Easter Sunday services
  • erhu (Chinese fiddle)
  • my vocal chords -- I was a soprano in the Magee Chamber Choir -- we did many cool things like tour Spain and perform with Rajaton and Eric Whitacre
  • clarinet (rather badly)
  • accordion (rather briefly)
