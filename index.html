I'm a computer science student at the University of British Columbia in stunningly beautiful Vancouver. On campus, you'll find me running around TA-ing for my favourite CS courses, or hanging out in the lab. Right now, I'm doing undergraduate research with Gail Murphy and Ron Garcia. I'm interested in understanding what makes software engineering so hard, and programming languages that make it easier.

3 years along in my university journey and I've eaten way too much pizza at hackathons, attended a host of techy conferences around North America, and been involved with UBC initiatives aimed at inspiring a new generation of both girl geeks and geek girls. I've been a software engineering intern at Tasktop and will be spending the upcoming summer (2017) at Microsoft in Redmond, WA. If you'd like to know more about my (work) experience, pop on over to my LinkedIn page.

While my computer is charging, I run long distances and finished my first half-marathon last year (my time was 2:06, and I plan on breaking 2:00 next time round!). I practice vinyasa yoga in the summer, bikram yoga in the winter and living room yoga all year round. I read and eat voraciously, and would consider myself a decent but minimalist cook -- if it takes more ingredients than I can carry on the bus, I probably won't be making it ;). In another life, I probably would have been a musician with all the instruments I picked up as a teenager (head on over to the about page for more). I have extremely itchy feet and travel as much as I possibly can.
